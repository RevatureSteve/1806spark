{{title}}
Components control views.
They are small pieces that render in our app.
They grant reusability and modularity.
Multiple components make our entire app.
{{subTitleDeclaration}}
Components are declared as an exported class.
They utilize the @Component decorator to define the metadata of the view.
The selector defines the HTML tag that we can use in our views.
The templateUrl defines the location of our Template.
The styleUrls define the locations of our multiple Styles.
They have their own lifecycle.
We can alter the lifecycle behavior by modifying its hooks.
{{subTitleLifecycle}}
Create.
Render.
Create and Render children.
Databound properties change.
Destroy.
{{subTitleHooks}}
ngOnChanges: called when an input binding value changes.
ngOnInit: after the first ngOnChanges.
ngDoCheck: after every run of change detection.
ngAfterContentInit: after component content is initialized.
ngAfterContentChecked: after every check of component content.
ngAfterViewInit: after component view(s) are initialized.
ngAfterViewChecked: after every check of a component's view(s).
ngOnDestroy: just before the component is destroyed.
