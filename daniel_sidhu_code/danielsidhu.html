Ramen

History

Japanese ramen first originated as a noodle dish in china and making it’s way through to Japan. Ramen was first called Shina Soba, which literally translated to “Chinese” Soba, was later changed to ramen due to it’s negative connotation. Today Japanese ramen comes in different flavors but usually will always follow somewhat of a guide line which is: fresh noodles, stock soup, flavorings, and toppings. These different guidelines still allow for a plethora of ramen and not only that but each district of Japan has different styles as well. Japan has over 80,000 types of ramens alone!
